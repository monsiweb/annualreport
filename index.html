Who Has Access to Financial Education in America Today?

In the 2020-2021 school year, nearly 7 out of 10 students across U.S. high schools had access to a stand-alone personal finance course. Of these students, about 2.4M (30%) of them were required to take the course prior to graduation

METHODOLOGY

  1. The data behind the report reflects 11,049 high schools and 11,752,118 high school students nationwide. The most recent NCES data estimates that there are 15,400,000 high school students in the U.S. today. Therefore, NGPF’s data captures 76.3% of public high school students in the U.S.
  2. Source: 77.8% of school level data is from Dr. Carly Urban and her research team’s latest study (2021) at Montana State University. The remaining 22.2% of school level data is from teacher reported data to NGPF, previous years of research from Dr. Carly Urban, and/or data collected as part of NGPF’s grant and research programs
  3. All ‘gold standard school’ data includes current gold standard schools as well as FUTURE gold standard schools. These are schools that have proven their intent to guarantee a course.
  4. A ‘guarantee’ state indicates those with state-wide personal finance graduation requirements. Currently, NGPF recognizes 5 states (AL, MO, TN, UT, VA) as ‘guarantee’ states and 2 states (NC, MS) as future ‘guarantee’ states as they have begun to transition schools
